Quirks & fixes.

The things that take a new tenant 3 months to figure out on their own.

The hot water tap in the master bath needs a firm twist — it sticks otherwise. Don't force it sideways; just twist it like you mean it. I asked the plumber to fix it twice; he said it would take replacing the whole unit and honestly it works fine once you know the move.

The generator switch is in the kitchen drawer (the one closest to the fridge), not the panel. Don't ask why — it was already like this when I moved in. Push the switch up for "estate gen", down for "own gen" (you won't need own gen; the estate one runs 24/7).

The balcony door slides a bit hard in dry weather. Lift slightly as you slide. In harmattan, run a candle along the track once a week — sounds ridiculous, the wax actually fixes it.

Front door key works better when you turn it fully right twice, then back left a quarter. Lock cylinder is a bit worn. Don't bother changing it; landlord will say "the door is fine".

The second guest bathroom shower head has weak pressure — there's a small black valve under the sink in that bathroom. If a guest is staying, twist the valve fully open before they shower, then close it again after. Otherwise pressure across the flat drops.

Fridge water dispenser stopped working a year ago. The fix is to defrost the freezer once a year — there's a hidden ice block behind the back panel. Engr. Sunday can do it for ₦7,000 or you can YouTube it; it's not that hard.

How rent & service are paid.

Save yourself the awkward first call.

Mr. Olabode prefers bank transfer, not cash. His GTB account ends in 8821 — he'll send you the full details after you've signed. Don't use Opay or Kuda for the rent payment; he won't trust it and will ask you to redo it via "a real bank".

Service charge is paid separately to the estate, not to him. Mr. Akin will give you the estate's account. Pay early in the year (before March) — by Q3 there's always a "diesel scarcity contribution" they try to add.
