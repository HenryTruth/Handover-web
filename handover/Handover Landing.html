How Handover works

A handover, not a hustle.

You're not booking a hotel. You're stepping into someone's lease. So we built it like the trade it actually is — direct, transparent, and fast.

01

Outgoing tenant lists

When you know you're leaving, post the place: photos, current rent, what's included, why you're moving. Takes about 8 minutes.

02

New tenant pays retainment

Interested? Pay a small retainment fee to secure your interest. It's held by Handover until the keys are handed over.

03

Outgoing tenant confirms

They review you, accept, and share the landlord's contact and all the house details — meter numbers, service charge, the gateman's WhatsApp.

04

You go direct with the landlord

You sign the lease yourself. No agent percentage. No middleman. Retainment is released to the outgoing tenant on move-in day.

Why retainment matters

Money on the table is the only filter that works.

In Nigerian rentals, "I'm interested" doesn't mean anything until naira moves. Retainment is the cost of being taken seriously — small enough to be reasonable, large enough to be real.

Held in escrow until you have the keys

Your retainment isn't sent to the outgoing tenant immediately. We hold it. They only get paid when you confirm you've signed with the landlord and moved in.

BVN-verified, NIN-verified, both sides

Outgoing tenants prove they live there with a utility bill in their name. New tenants verify identity before paying. We see both names.

If the handover falls through, you get refunded

Landlord refuses you? Tenant pulls out? Place isn't what was advertised? File a dispute within 72 hours. Refund processed in 5 working days.

Common questions

Things people ask before their first handover.

Is this legal? Don't I need the landlord's permission to hand over?

Most Nigerian leases either explicitly allow tenant-introduced replacements or are silent on it — which means you're free to recommend someone. Handover is a recommendation engine with money on top. The landlord still has final say and signs the new lease directly.

How much is the retainment fee?

The outgoing tenant sets it. Typical range is 0.5%–1.5% of annual rent — so ₦15k for a ₦950k self-con, ₦80k for a ₦12M duplex. It signals serious interest. The full amount goes to the outgoing tenant after a successful handover.

What does Handover charge?

₦2,500 flat from the new tenant when retainment is paid. That's it. No commission, no percentage of rent, no listing fee for outgoing tenants.

Can more than one person pay retainment on the same place?

No — only one retainment slot per listing, first-come. If you pay and the outgoing tenant doesn't confirm you within 48 hours, you're refunded and the slot reopens.

What happens to my retainment if the landlord rejects me?

Full refund. If you can show the landlord said no after the outgoing tenant introduced you, we release your retainment back within 5 working days. The outgoing tenant doesn't get paid for an introduction that didn't land.
