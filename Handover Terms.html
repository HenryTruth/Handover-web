2. The Platform

Handover is a peer-to-peer marketplace that connects outgoing tenants with incoming tenants for apartment transitions across Nigeria. Handover is not an estate agent, a property manager, a landlord, or a party to any tenancy agreement.

Handover provides the technology, the payment infrastructure, the escrow service, and the dispute resolution process. Handover does not own, manage, or inspect any property listed on the Platform. All listings are created by individual users who are themselves tenants.

By using the Platform, you acknowledge that any tenancy agreement you enter into is directly between you and the landlord — not between you and Handover.

5. Listings

Who can list

Any verified user who is currently a tenant in a residential property in Nigeria may create a listing for that property. You may only list a flat in which you genuinely reside or for which you hold a valid tenancy agreement.

What listings must include

A listing must accurately represent the property. It must include:

  • Honest photos of every room and the exterior.
  • The property type, number of bedrooms and bathrooms, and key amenities.
  • The current annual rent charged by the landlord.
  • The retainment amount set by the Outgoing Tenant.
  • The anticipated available-from date.
  • A personal write-up about the flat written in the Outgoing Tenant's own words.

Accuracy

You must not misrepresent the condition, size, amenities, location, or price of a listed property. Listings must not contain fabricated photos, stock imagery, or images of a different property. Misrepresentation is a ground for dispute, account suspension, and forfeiture of the retainment.

One retainment slot per listing

Only one Incoming Tenant may hold the retainment slot on a listing at any time. Once a retainment is paid, the listing is marked as reserved. If the Outgoing Tenant does not confirm the Incoming Tenant within 48 hours, the retainment is refunded and the slot reopens automatically.

Removing a listing

You may remove an active listing at any time before a retainment is paid. Once a retainment is paid, a listing cannot be deleted without going through the dispute or refund process.

6. Retainment

What retainment is

Retainment is a holding fee paid by the Incoming Tenant to signal serious intent and secure the listing. It is not an agency fee, a finder's fee, a security deposit, or rent. It is a structured trust signal — small enough to be reasonable, large enough to be real.

Who sets it

The Outgoing Tenant sets the retainment amount when creating a listing. Typical retainments range from 0.5% to 1.5% of annual rent. Handover does not set or regulate the retainment amount, but reserves the right to flag or remove listings with amounts that are disproportionate.

What happens to it

The retainment is held in escrow by Handover from the moment of payment. It is not accessible to the Outgoing Tenant during this period. The retainment is released to the Outgoing Tenant only after the Incoming Tenant confirms Key Receipt in the app. If the handover does not proceed, the retainment is either refunded to the Incoming Tenant or remains locked pending a dispute outcome.

Important: Retainment payments made outside the Handover Platform — via WhatsApp, direct bank transfer, or any other channel — are not protected by our escrow. Handover takes no responsibility for off-platform payments and cannot facilitate disputes or refunds for them.

7. The Handover Process

Once a Retainment is paid, both parties are guided through a structured five-step process inside the app:

  1. Retainment held — Confirmed and secured in escrow.
  2. Landlord confirmation — The Outgoing Tenant (or landlord) formally accepts the Incoming Tenant within an agreed window. Acceptance is the landlord's decision alone.
  3. Inspection and agreement — Both parties arrange a walkthrough of the property.
  4. Tenancy agreement signed — A new tenancy agreement is signed directly between the Incoming Tenant and the landlord. Handover may provide template documentation but is not a party to this agreement.
  5. Move-in and Key Confirmation — The Incoming Tenant takes possession and confirms key receipt in the app. This confirmation triggers the release of the escrow to the Outgoing Tenant.

Both parties communicate exclusively through the in-app messaging system until both sides are comfortable sharing personal contact details. Handover retains records of all in-app communication for the purposes of dispute resolution.

8. The Handover Manual

The Outgoing Tenant may write a Handover Manual for their listing at any time after creating it. The manual is a personal document covering appliances, utilities, building access, neighbourhood notes, and anything else a new tenant should know about the flat.

The manual belongs to a single listing. It is never reused across multiple handovers. Once the handover is complete, the manual is shared with the confirmed Incoming Tenant and remains accessible to them.

The Outgoing Tenant is responsible for the accuracy of the manual. Handover does not verify the contents. Deliberate misinformation in the manual is grounds for a dispute.

9. Escrow & Payments

Handover holds the retainment in escrow using licensed payment infrastructure operating under applicable Nigerian financial regulations.

The escrow is released to the Outgoing Tenant only when the Incoming Tenant confirms Key Receipt in the app. This is the sole trigger for release under normal circumstances.

Escrow funds are not interest-bearing. Handover does not benefit financially from holding the escrow beyond the processing fees disclosed at the time of payment.

By paying a retainment, the Incoming Tenant authorises Handover to hold the funds under the escrow conditions described here.

10. Refunds

When a refund is available

Refunds are available to the Incoming Tenant at any time before Key Confirmation. Once the Incoming Tenant confirms they have received the keys, the escrow is released and the transaction is complete. A refund cannot be requested after Key Confirmation — there are no escrow funds remaining to return.

How refunds work

The Incoming Tenant requests a refund through the app. No approval from the Outgoing Tenant is required. The refund is processed to the original payment method within 24 hours.

Outgoing Tenant's protection

The Outgoing Tenant cannot block a refund. Their only protection is to raise a dispute before the refund is processed. A dispute immediately locks the escrow and pauses any pending refund. A Handover mediator is then assigned to resolve the situation. If the Outgoing Tenant does not raise a dispute in time, the refund proceeds automatically.

Refunds and active disputes

Refund requests interact with disputes in three scenarios:

  • No active dispute: The refund is processed automatically within 24 hours.
  • Active dispute raised by the Outgoing Tenant: The mediator holds both the dispute and the refund request and determines the outcome. The Incoming Tenant is not penalised for a dispute they did not initiate.
  • Active dispute raised by the Incoming Tenant: The Incoming Tenant cannot run a dispute and a refund request simultaneously. The app will require them to choose — withdraw the dispute and proceed with the refund, or keep the dispute open. Only one claim may be active at a time.

11. Disputes

When to raise a dispute

Either party may raise a dispute when the handover does not proceed as expected. Common grounds include:

For the Incoming Tenant:

  • Keys were not handed over.
  • The flat condition does not match the listing description or photos.
  • The landlord refused to accept the Incoming Tenant after introduction.
  • The move-in date was changed without notice.

For the Outgoing Tenant:

  • Keys have been physically handed over but the Incoming Tenant has not confirmed in the app.
  • The Incoming Tenant is unresponsive and has not completed the move-in checklist.
  • A refund request is not valid because the Incoming Tenant has taken possession.

What raising a dispute does

Filing a dispute immediately locks the escrow. Any automatic release and any pending refund are paused. A Handover mediator is assigned within 24 hours and contacts both parties through the app.

For Outgoing Tenants — key protection: Once you physically hand over the keys, ask the Incoming Tenant to confirm receipt in the app immediately. That confirmation is what releases your escrow payment. If they refuse to confirm or go silent after taking the keys, raise a dispute right away. Waiting will not help you — a dispute raised after the Incoming Tenant has requested a refund may still be overridden if the mediator determines the keys were genuinely withheld.

Resolving a dispute

A dispute does not close when one party declares it resolved. Both parties must independently confirm resolution. The process is:

  1. Either party presses Mark as resolved.
  2. The other party receives an immediate notification.
  3. The other party must press Confirm resolution to agree.
  4. Only when both sides have confirmed does the dispute close.

When a dispute closes, the escrow is released to the Outgoing Tenant. Disputes are about accountability and process — they are not a mechanism for returning money to the Incoming Tenant. Refunds are handled separately.

If the second party disagrees with the proposed resolution, they may decline and reopen the case with the mediator. The dispute remains open and funds remain locked until both sides agree.

Incoming Tenant's right to appeal after resolution

Where a dispute was raised by the Outgoing Tenant specifically to block a refund request, and both parties subsequently mark the dispute as resolved — causing the escrow to be released to the Outgoing Tenant by default — the Incoming Tenant retains the right to appeal to Handover if they believe the resolution was unjust.

This right applies specifically where the Incoming Tenant has evidence that the Outgoing Tenant acted in bad faith or breached their obligations under these Terms. Grounds for appeal include, but are not limited to:

  • Rent inflation — the rent demanded by the landlord was materially higher than the annual rent stated on the listing at the time the retainment was paid.
  • Listing discrepancy — the condition, amenities, size, or features of the property were materially different from what was described or photographed in the listing.
  • Breach of agreement — the Outgoing Tenant failed to fulfil their obligations in the handover process in a way that directly caused the incoming tenant loss.

How to appeal: Contact Handover Support at support@handoverapp.ng within 14 days of the dispute closing. Include the transaction reference, a clear description of your concern, and any supporting evidence (photos, screenshots, messages, or documents). Appeals submitted after 14 days will not be considered.

Handover will review the appeal independently. Our review will examine the original listing, all in-app communications, any media or documentation submitted by the Incoming Tenant, and any response provided by the Outgoing Tenant. Both parties will be given a reasonable opportunity to present their position before a determination is made.

If Handover determines the appeal is valid — meaning the Outgoing Tenant's conduct or misrepresentation is found to be substantiated — Handover will:

  1. Process a full refund of the retainment to the Incoming Tenant within 5 working days.
  2. Formally close the dispute in the Incoming Tenant's favour.
  3. Take appropriate action against the Outgoing Tenant's account, which may include a permanent ban from the Platform.

If the appeal is not upheld, the original dispute outcome stands and no refund will be issued.

Important: This appeal process is not a general right to undo any resolved dispute. It applies only where there is clear evidence of misrepresentation or bad faith by the Outgoing Tenant. Disagreements about subjective preferences — such as the style of the flat, the neighbourhood, or a change of mind — are not valid grounds for appeal. Handover's determination on an appeal is final.
