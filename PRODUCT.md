# Handover — Product Overview

**Person to person · Nigeria**

---

## What is Handover?

Handover is a mobile app that connects people leaving a rented apartment directly with people looking for one — no agent, no agency fee. Instead of walking into a property through a third party who knows nothing about the place, the incoming tenant hears from the person who actually lived there. The outgoing tenant writes the listing, sets the price, and personally hands over the flat.

The name is the whole idea: one person hands over their home to the next.

---

## The Problem It Solves

Renting in Nigeria is broken in a specific way. Agents control access to most available apartments, charge fees on both sides, and disappear after the transaction. The person who knows the flat — which switch controls the borehole, where the parking spot is, how the neighbor upstairs behaves — is cut out of the process entirely.

Handover removes the agent from the middle and replaces them with a structured, trust-based process between real people.

---

## Who Uses It

**Outgoing tenants** — people whose lease is ending (or who are leaving before it ends) and want to find a good person to take over their flat. They create the listing, set the retainment amount, communicate with interested parties, and receive their money once the new tenant confirms they have the keys.

**Incoming tenants** — people looking for a place to rent who want to deal directly with someone who lived there, see honest photos, read a real story about the apartment, and understand what they're moving into before they pay.

---

## How It Works

### 1. Browse
The incoming tenant opens the app and browses available flats filtered by city. Each listing shows the property type, price per year, location, and a small retainment amount — a deposit that holds the flat and signals genuine interest.

Filters are available for property type (self-con, mini-flat, 2-bedroom, 3-bedroom, duplex, penthouse), amenities (borehole, inverter, parking, furnished, BQ, prepaid meter), and move-in window.

A map view shows nearby listings by price.

### 2. The Listing
Each listing is written by the outgoing tenant themselves. It includes:

- Photos of every room
- A personal write-up about the flat — what they loved, what to know
- Detailed features (number of beds, baths, security, AC, parking, etc.)
- The annual rent and retainment amount
- An "Available from" date

### 3. Retainment
When a tenant is interested, they pay a retainment — a small holding fee (distinct from the full rent). This is not an agency fee; it goes into escrow and is either counted toward the first rent payment or refunded if the deal does not go through.

The retainment is the signal. It removes tyre-kickers and tells the outgoing tenant this person is serious.

Payment is made through the app via bank transfer or card.

### 4. The Handover Flow
Once retainment is paid, the app guides both parties through a step-by-step process:

1. **Retainment paid** — confirmed and held securely
2. **Landlord confirmation** — the outgoing tenant (or landlord) formally accepts within a set window
3. **Inspection & agreement** — a walkthrough is scheduled between both parties
4. **Tenancy agreement signed** — legal documentation handled through the app
5. **Move-in & key collection** — the incoming tenant confirms they have the keys

Funds are released to the outgoing tenant only after the incoming tenant confirms key collection. Until that point, the money is protected.

### 5. In-App Messaging
Both parties communicate directly through the app throughout the process. There are no numbers exchanged until both sides are comfortable. The chat handles logistics — inspection times, questions about the flat, move-in arrangements.

### 6. The Handover Manual
The outgoing tenant writes a manual for their flat. It is one of the most distinctive features of the app. The manual belongs to the listing — one manual per listing, never shared across multiple handovers. The outgoing tenant can start writing it at any point after creating their listing; they do not need to wait for an incoming tenant to appear. The manual covers:

- **Appliances & fixtures** — how things work, where switches are, the quirks
- **Building & security** — gate codes, security schedules, spare key locations, building management contacts
- **Water & utilities** — prepaid meter, borehole, water pressure, hot water
- **Neighborhood notes** — good spots nearby, parking realities, noise expectations
- **What stays with the flat** — furniture, appliances, and anything else that comes with it

The manual is written in plain, personal language. It is a letter from one tenant to the next.

### 7. Move-In Checklist
On move-in day, the app surfaces a checklist to make sure everything is properly handed over:

- Flat inspection completed
- Tenancy agreement signed
- Keys collected (both sets)
- Prepaid meter topped up
- Caretaker introduced
- Move-in photos taken

When the incoming tenant confirms key receipt, the escrow releases to the outgoing tenant automatically.

### 8. Disputes

**Raising a dispute**

If something goes wrong — the landlord refuses to accept, the keys were never handed over, the flat condition does not match the listing, or the move-in date was changed without notice — either party can raise a dispute through the app. The raising party selects a reason, adds context, and submits. The retainment is immediately locked in escrow and the automatic 72-hour release is paused. A Handover mediator is assigned within 24 hours and contacts both parties.

**Reasons available to the incoming tenant:**
- Keys were not handed over
- Flat condition does not match the listing
- Landlord refused to accept
- Move-in date was changed without notice
- Other (free text)

**Reasons available to the outgoing tenant:**
- Keys handed over, no confirmation — the incoming tenant physically has the keys but has not confirmed in the app
- Incoming tenant is unresponsive — not responding and has not completed the move-in checklist
- Refund request is not valid — the tenant has taken possession of the flat and a refund is not justified
- Other (free text)

**Key handover protection for outgoing tenants**

The app reminds the outgoing tenant of a critical step: once the keys are physically handed over, the incoming tenant must confirm receipt inside the app. That confirmation is what releases the escrow payment to the outgoing tenant. If the incoming tenant refuses to confirm or goes silent after receiving the keys, the outgoing tenant should raise a dispute immediately. Raising a dispute pauses any refund requests and brings in a mediator to resolve the standoff.

The exact guidance shown to outgoing tenants in the app: *"Once you hand over the keys, ask the incoming tenant to confirm in the app. Their confirmation is what releases your payment — if they don't, raise a dispute immediately to protect your payment."*

**Resolving a dispute**

A dispute does not close when one party says it is fine. Both the outgoing tenant and the incoming tenant must independently mark it as resolved.

The flow works like this:

1. Either party presses **Mark as resolved** on their side of the app.
2. The other party receives a notification immediately.
3. The other party must open the dispute and press **Confirm resolution** to agree.
4. Only when both sides have confirmed does the dispute officially close.

When a dispute resolves, the escrow is always released to the outgoing tenant. Disputes are about accountability and process — they are not a mechanism for returning money to the incoming tenant. If the incoming tenant wants their money back, that is a refund request, which is a separate process entirely.

If the second party disagrees with the proposed resolution, they can decline and reopen the case with the mediator rather than confirming. The dispute remains open and funds remain locked until both sides confirm. This two-party confirmation applies only to closing a dispute — a refund request is independent and does not require the outgoing tenant's agreement.

---

### 9. Refunds

A refund returns the retainment to the incoming tenant. It is entirely separate from a dispute — a dispute never results in a refund, and a refund is not a dispute.

**Standard refund**

The incoming tenant requests a refund through the app. The money is returned to their original payment method within 24 hours — no approval from the outgoing tenant is required.

The outgoing tenant has no power to reject a refund. Their only protection is to raise a dispute before the refund is processed. A dispute immediately locks the escrow and pauses any pending refund, bringing in a mediator to resolve the situation. If the outgoing tenant does nothing, the refund goes through.

**Refunds are only available before key receipt is confirmed.** Once the incoming tenant confirms they have received the keys, the escrow releases to the outgoing tenant and the transaction is complete. A refund cannot be requested after that point — there is nothing left in escrow to return. The refund option is removed from the app the moment key receipt is confirmed.

**Refunds and disputes interact in three ways:**

**Case 1 — No active dispute**
The incoming tenant requests a refund directly. It is processed within 24 hours automatically. The outgoing tenant is notified but cannot block it.

**Case 2 — A dispute is active, but it was raised by the outgoing tenant**
The incoming tenant did not choose to be in a dispute. They can still request a refund independently. The mediator holds both the dispute and the refund request and determines the outcome. The incoming tenant is not penalised for a dispute they did not initiate.

**Case 3 — A dispute is active and the incoming tenant raised it**
The incoming tenant cannot run both a dispute and a refund request at the same time — they are two competing claims on the same escrow. If they request a refund while their own dispute is open, the app will offer them a choice: withdraw the dispute and proceed with the refund, or keep the dispute open and drop the refund request. Only one claim can be active at a time. If they choose to withdraw the dispute and request the refund, the dispute is closed and the refund is processed within 24 hours.

## Listing a Flat

Outgoing tenants create a listing in four steps:

1. **Details** — property type, location, number of beds and baths, available date, amenities
2. **Pricing** — annual rent and retainment amount
3. **Your story** — a personal write-up about the flat, the building, and the neighborhood
4. **Photos** — images of every room, uploaded directly from the phone

Once live, the listing appears in Browse for anyone searching in that area. The tenant can manage all their active listings from the My Listings screen and track how many people are interested.

## Roles and Identity

A user's role — outgoing tenant or incoming tenant — is always relative to a specific property handover, never to their account globally. The same person can be the outgoing tenant on one handover (they are leaving a flat) and the incoming tenant on another handover (they are taking over a different flat) at exactly the same time.

A user's role on a given handover is determined by a simple fact: whoever created the listing is the outgoing tenant. Whoever paid the retainment is the incoming tenant. The backend holds this and knows which role the currently logged-in user holds when they open any screen related to that handover. The app receives the role as part of the handover data and uses it to decide what copy, actions, and options to show — the same screen can present an entirely different experience depending on who is looking at it.

No user can ever be both the outgoing and incoming tenant on the same property. They cannot list a flat they are simultaneously retaining.

---

## Trust & Verification

Users verify their identity through:

- NIN (National Identification Number)
- Selfie check
- Phone number confirmation

Verified profiles are marked with a badge visible on listings and in chat. This creates accountability on both sides of the transaction.

---

## Key Principles

**Person to person.** Every listing is written by someone who lived in the flat. Every transaction is between two individuals, not between a tenant and an institution.

**Protected money.** Retainment is held in escrow from the moment it is paid until the incoming tenant confirms the keys are in their hands. Neither party can access it in the interim.

**The story matters.** Listings are not just specs. The outgoing tenant writes about the flat in their own words — what they will miss, what a new tenant should know, what makes the place worth taking. This is not a feature; it is the product.

**Honest handover.** The manual is what makes the transition real. A new tenant should not spend their first week confused about how the water pump works or which parking spot is theirs. The outgoing tenant's knowledge of the flat does not leave with them — it gets passed on.
